# Stoic Mirror Branding Guide

## Philosophy & Inspiration

Stoic Mirror draws inspiration from the great Stoic philosophers: **Marcus Aurelius**, **Epictetus**, and **Seneca**. Each represents different aspects of mindfulness and self-reflection:

- **Marcus Aurelius** (121-180 AD) - The Emperor-Philosopher
  - Known for: Daily journaling, self-reflection, duty and virtue
  - Quote: "You have power over your mind‚Äînot outside events. Realize this, and you will find strength."
  - Represents: **Today Tab** - Daily routine, commitment, discipline

- **Epictetus** (50-135 AD) - The Freed Slave-Philosopher
  - Known for: What's in our control, practical wisdom, acceptance
  - Quote: "We cannot choose our external circumstances, but we can always choose how we respond to them."
  - Represents: **Journal Tab** - Reflection, finding what's in our control

- **Seneca** (4 BC-65 AD) - The Statesman-Philosopher
  - Known for: Letters, practical philosophy, managing time and emotions
  - Quote: "It is not that we have a short time to live, but that we waste a lot of it."
  - Represents: **Tasks Tab** - Time management, productivity

- **Zeno of Citium** (334-262 BC) - The Founder
  - Known for: Founded Stoicism, the porch (Stoa Poikile)
  - Represents: **Weekly Tab** - Overview, philosophy foundation

## Design Elements

### Color Palette
- **Primary**: Deep classical stone/bronze tones (#8B7355, #A0826D) - references classical sculptures
- **Accent**: Gold/Amber (#D4AF37, #F4A460) - classical Greek/Roman metalwork
- **Background**: Warm marble whites (#F5F5DC, #FAF0E6) and dark slate (#2C2C2C) - classical architecture
- **Text**: Deep charcoal (#1A1A1A) - ancient ink

### Typography
- **Headings**: Serif font (reminiscent of classical inscriptions)
- **Body**: Clean sans-serif (Inter - modern, readable)
- **Quotes**: Italic serif for philosopher quotes

### Iconography Concepts

#### App Icon
- **Option 1**: Classical bust silhouette (Marcus Aurelius)
- **Option 2**: Greek/Roman column with mirror reflection
- **Option 3**: Classical mask (comedy/tragedy) representing reflection
- **Style**: Minimalist line art or low-poly classical sculpture

- **Classical/Minimal**: Inspired by Greco-Roman art but modernized

## Brand Voice

### Tone
- **Wise but approachable**: Not preachy, but grounded
- **Direct yet warm**: Like a Stoic friend, not a lecture
- **Practical**: Focused on what you can do, not abstract philosophy
- **Reflective**: Encourages self-awareness without judgment

### Messaging Examples
- "What's in your control today?" (Epictetus influence)
- "Reflect on the day" (Marcus Aurelius influence)
- "Time is your most valuable resource" (Seneca influence)
- "Know thyself" (Classical philosophy)

## Implementation

### App Icon Generation
1. Start with classical bust silhouette (Marcus Aurelius or generic philosopher)
2. Add subtle mirror effect or reflection
3. Use bronze/stone color palette
4. Keep minimal - recognizable at small sizes
5. Export as .icns (macOS), .ico (Windows), PNG (various sizes)

### Tab Icons
- Use consistent style: line art or minimalist classical symbols
- Bronze/gold accent color
- Clear, recognizable at small sizes

### Onboarding
- Include quotes from philosophers
- Reference Stoic principles in feature explanations
- "Inspired by the wisdom of Marcus Aurelius, Epictetus, and Seneca"

### App Metadata
- Description: "A mindful productivity app inspired by Stoic philosophy"
- Keywords: stoicism, productivity, journaling, mindfulness, philosophy
